## Bodies, Identity, and the Privatization of Politics

### Lecture Purpose
This lecture explores the sexual revolution as a broader cultural shift in which Americans redefined freedom, authority, and identity at a moment of declining trust in public institutions.

---

### I. The Preconditions of Sexual Change
- Birth control pill and reproductive autonomy
- Postwar affluence and generational rebellion
- Decline of deference to traditional moral authorities

### II. Feminism and Gender Roles
- Second-wave feminism and workplace equality
- Critique of domestic confinement
- Reframing personal experience as political

### III. Gay Liberation and Identity Politics
- Stonewall and the emergence of visibility politics
- Sexual orientation as identity rather than behavior
- Challenges to heteronormative assumptions

### IV. From Public Politics to Personal Freedom
- Retreat from institutional trust
- Emphasis on self-expression, authenticity, and choice
- Culture as the new battleground

### V. The Political Context
- Law-and-order politics and cultural backlash
- Richard Nixon and the politics of moral anxiety
- The roots of modern culture wars

### Key Takeaway
As faith in public authority declined, Americans increasingly relocated freedom and meaning into personal life, permanently reshaping politics.

# Chapter 28 — America at Midcentury, 1953–1963  
## Module Introduction

This module explores a period remembered for stability and prosperity while revealing the tensions beneath that surface.

Rather than repeating the textbook narrative, these lectures emphasize interpretation—how Americans experienced power, democracy, and rupture.

---

## Lecture Arc

### Lecture 1 — Television Changes Everything
Media transforms political authority and trust.

### Lecture 2 — Civil Rights as a Global Legitimacy Crisis
Civil rights reframed as a Cold War credibility problem.

### Lecture 3 — November 1963: Memory, Shock, and the End of Confidence
Assassination as symbolic rupture and historical memory.

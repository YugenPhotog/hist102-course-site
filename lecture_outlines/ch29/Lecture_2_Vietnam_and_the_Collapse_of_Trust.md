# Lecture 2 — Vietnam and the Collapse of Trust
## War, Credibility, and Democratic Crisis

### Lecture Purpose
This lecture analyzes the Vietnam War as a crisis of credibility that shattered public trust in government institutions and permanently altered American political culture.

---

### I. Vietnam as a Political War
- Cold War containment logic and escalation
- Absence of clear objectives or achievable victory
- Reliance on metrics: body counts, kill ratios, progress reports

### II. The Credibility Gap
- Official optimism vs. battlefield realities
- Pentagon Papers and exposure of systematic deception
- Television and the intimacy of modern warfare

### III. Vietnam and Protest Culture
- Student activism and the antiwar movement
- Connections to civil rights and New Left politics
- Kent State and the normalization of domestic conflict

### IV. Lyndon Johnson and the Tragedy of Power
- Great Society ambitions undermined by war spending
- Political constraints and fear of appearing weak
- Vietnam as a lesson in unintended consequences

### V. Long-Term Consequences
- Permanent erosion of trust in government narratives
- Rise of skepticism toward expertise and authority
- Vietnam as the turning point from confidence to cynicism

### Key Takeaway
Vietnam transformed political disagreement into disbelief, fundamentally altering Americans’ relationship to their government.

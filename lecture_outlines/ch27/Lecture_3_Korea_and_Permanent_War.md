
# Lecture 3 — Containment Goes Hot: Korea and the Normalization of Permanent War (1950–1953)

## Lecture Purpose
This lecture examines the Korean War as the moment containment becomes militarized and normalized, locking the United States into undeclared wars, global commitments, and permanent military readiness.

## Key Questions
- Why did Korea matter so much to U.S. policymakers?
- How did Korea change the meaning of containment?
- What precedents did the war establish?

## I. Asia After 1949
- Communist China and regional anxiety
- Nuclear weapons and escalation fears
- U.S. commitment to credibility

## II. Origins of the Korean War
- Division of Korea after WWII
- Civil war becomes international conflict
- UN intervention under U.S. leadership

## III. NSC-68
- Perception of total threat
- Call for massive military expansion
- Fear as policy logic

## IV. Truman and MacArthur
- Disagreements over escalation
- Civilian control of the military
- Limits of presidential authority during crisis

## V. War Without Declaration
- Congressional sidelining
- Massive civilian casualties
- Racialized enemy perceptions

## VI. Korea as Precedent
- Permanent troop deployments
- Defense spending normalization
- Template for future Cold War wars

## Takeaway
Korea transforms containment from doctrine into permanent military reality.

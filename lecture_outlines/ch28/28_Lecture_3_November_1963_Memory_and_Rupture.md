# Lecture 3 — November 1963: Memory, Shock, and the End of Confidence  
**Chapter 28: America at Midcentury, 1953–1963**

## Central Question
Why do some moments feel like endings even when policy does not immediately change?

## Framing Argument
The assassination of John F. Kennedy changed how Americans experienced trust, time, and the future.

---

## I. Pre-Assassination Confidence
- Faith in institutions and expertise
- Belief in managed progress

## II. The Assassination as Media Event
- Shared national trauma via television
- Continuous coverage creates collective shock

## III. Why It Felt Like an Ending
- Loss of confidence and continuity
- Safety and stability questioned

## IV. Memory and Periodization
- Historical meaning constructed in hindsight
- November 1963 becomes symbolic boundary

## V. Aftermath
- Erosion of institutional trust
- Cultural shift toward skepticism

## Concluding Takeaway
The assassination revealed the fragility of midcentury confidence.
